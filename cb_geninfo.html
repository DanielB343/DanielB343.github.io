General Cat Info

What are cats?

Cats are one of the most common pets to have in the world, however cats as a species did not start out domesticated. These animals started as normal wild animals that hunt and kill others for food and usually go in packs. Many features of these wild cats are still in the house cat today like there good agile or power which still makes good hunters of smaller animals.

The first evidence of domestication with cats was thousands of years ago in Europe and Egypt. Cats have always been good partners to humans even back then which lead to now where there are around 46.5 million people who own a cat. Domesticated cats now come in many different shapes sizes and colors and this website is here to help you learn about all of them.

About Cats

The size of cats

Domestic cats are usually small animal species especially compared to its wild counterpart. Cats are usuallly around 18 inches long and 9-10 inches tall they also weigh around 10 pounds. Male cats are usually bigger than females but it is usually by not that much. The breed also has a factor in the size of the cat which this website will go into more detail in the pages for these breeds.

The color of cats

There are many colors that a cats can be which can depend on the breed of the cat. The most common colors for cats are white, black, red, brown, and gray with some unique colors like fawn and cinnamon. There are also many color coat patterns that a cat can had which can be just one solid color or even a mix of multiple colors.

The act of cats

Cats mostly speak through acts rather than words and cat can tell you a lot of things based on how they act and their body language. Cats use their eyes for example to show there affection to their owners by blinking slowly or by head-butting you. Cats show anger by rising their backs with hiss or a growling sound to go with it. A cat can show fear or stress by the widening of their eyes and hiding under things like beds. These are all examples of acts and body language being used as a form of communication.

The caring of cats

If you going to have a cat you going to need to know how to care for the cat. Got to make the cat is well fed, groomed, and bathed however there is also a social aspect to caring for the cat. If you don't give the proper attention it needs it will cause it to be lazier it could harm the cat's health. Vet visits are also important to see if any is wrong with the cat and if anything can be done to help it. With all these in mind if you take good proper care of your cat it might live a longer life than normal.

FAQ

How many breeds are there?
It's hard to tell the exact number but there are around 40-70 different cat breeds in the world depending on who you ask.
Is it ok to take a cat outside?
It depends on the cat however you need to be careful when doing so that the cat does not go missing or get attacked by it's predators.
How do a create a bond with my cat?
The best way to build a bond with a cat is to play and train for a couple of times a day. Not only does help build a bond with them but it teaches the cat proper manners or even some cool tricks if done long enough.
What is a calico cat?
Calico cats are a cat with three different colors. The common color for calico cats is mainly a white cat with big black and brown spots but this can be different depending on the breed.
Should I get a yonger or older cat?
Both young and old cats are valid options to pick. Younger cats are easier to train but it doesn't mean older cats are a bad pick. It all depends on what you want in a cat.
What's the best food to feed a cat?
Dry cat food is better for a cat's teeth, however, so can wet food is fine for a cat. You have to make sure that what food you getting is good for your cat and make sure to not feed it too much.
Where should I adopted a cat?
Look up your local adoption center near you. If they have a website you should check and see if they have the cat listed there if not just go there and check for yourself. Another way is to look for people who have cats up for adoption online. These usually have detailed information about cats and the location to adopt them.
